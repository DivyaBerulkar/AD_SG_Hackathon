*Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence:*

1. *"A Mathematical Theory of Communication"* by Claude Shannon - This paper laid the foundation for information theory and digital communication.

2. *"Computing Machinery and Intelligence"* by Alan Turing - Turing's seminal work on artificial intelligence introduced the concept of the Turing Test.

3. *"PageRank: Bringing Order to the Web"* by Larry Page and Sergey Brin - The paper that introduced the PageRank algorithm, the foundation of Google's search engine.

*Physics:*

1. *"On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies"* by Albert Einstein - Einstein's theory of special relativity revolutionized our understanding of space and time.

2. *"The Theory of Everything"* by Stephen Hawking - Hawking's groundbreaking paper discussed the singularity theorems in black hole physics.

*Biology and Medicine:*

1. *"The Structure of DNA"* by James Watson and Francis Crick - This paper famously described the double-helix structure of DNA, the molecule of life.

2. *"The Central Dogma of Molecular Biology"* by Francis Crick - Crick's paper introduced the concept of how genetic information flows within a biological system.

*Economics:*

1. *"The Economic Consequences of the Peace"* by John Maynard Keynes - Keynes' work on the economic implications of the Treaty of Versailles had a significant impact on economic policy.

2. *"The Use of Knowledge in Society"* by Friedrich Hayek - Hayek's paper on the role of prices and market mechanisms in economic coordination.

*Mathematics:*

1. *"On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"* by Alan Turing - Turing's paper that introduced the concept of Turing machines and computational complexity.

2. *"A New Proof of the Four-Color Theorem"* by Kenneth Appel and Wolfgang Haken - This paper provided a solution to the famous mathematical problem known as the Four-Color Theorem.
