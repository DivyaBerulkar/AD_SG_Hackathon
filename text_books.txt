*General Reference:*

1. "The Chicago Manual of Style" by The University of Chicago Press
   - A comprehensive guide to writing, editing, and publishing research papers and documents.

*Science and Engineering:*

2. "Physics for Scientists and Engineers" by Paul A. Tipler and Gene Mosca
   - A widely used physics textbook covering classical mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and more.

3. "Calculus: Early Transcendentals" by James Stewart
   - A popular calculus textbook that covers single-variable and multi-variable calculus.

4. "Introduction to Algorithms" by Thomas H. Cormen, Charles E. Leiserson, Ronald L. Rivest, and Clifford Stein
   - A classic textbook for computer science and algorithms.

5. "Fundamentals of Electric Circuits" by Charles K. Alexander and Matthew N.O. Sadiku
   - A comprehensive book on electrical engineering and circuit analysis.

*Social Sciences and Humanities:*

6. "The Craft of Research" by Wayne C. Booth, Gregory G. Colomb, and Joseph M. Williams
   - A guide to research methods, writing, and critical thinking for social science and humanities researchers.

7. "The Elements of Style" by William Strunk Jr. and E.B. White
   - A classic guide to English grammar and writing style.

*Mathematics:*

8. "Linear Algebra and Its Applications" by David C. Lay, Steven R. Lay, and Judi J. McDonald
   - A widely used textbook for linear algebra.

9. "Introduction to Probability" by Joseph K. Blitzstein and Jessica Hwang
   - A comprehensive introduction to probability theory.

*Medicine and Life Sciences:*

10. "Principles of Neural Science" by Eric R. Kandel, James H. Schwartz, and Thomas M. Jessell
    - A comprehensive neuroscience textbook.

11. "Molecular Biology of the Cell" by Bruce Alberts, Alexander Johnson, Julian Lewis, Martin Raff, Keith Roberts, and Peter Walter
    - A widely used textbook in cell biology and molecular biology.

*Business and Economics:*

12. "Principles of Economics" by N. Gregory Mankiw
    - A popular introductory economics textbook.

13. "The Lean Startup" by Eric Ries
    - A guide for entrepreneurs and researchers on building successful startups.
